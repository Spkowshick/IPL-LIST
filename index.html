NESTED LIST

  1. History


    1. Background

      2. The Indian Cricket League (ICL) was launched in April 2007 by the Essel Group and IL&FS but was not recognised by the BCCI or ICC.
      In response, BCCI imposed lifetime bans on players joining the ICL and increased reward structures for its own tournaments

    2. Foundation

      3. The league was officially launched in 2007, with the first season held in 2008.
      Lalit Modi, then Vice President of BCCI, played a major role in setting it up.
      Eight franchise teams were auctioned, based on cities.

    3. Expansions and termination

      4. Over the years, teams were added (e.g., Pune Warriors India, Kochi Tuskers Kerala) and removed due to financial or legal reasons.
      Chennai Super Kings and Rajasthan Royals were suspended in 2015, but returned later.

  2. Administration


    3. Explains the governing bodies (like BCCI, IPL Governing Council) and key figures managing the IPL.


  3. Organisation


    1. Tournament format
      • League + Playoffs format.
      • 10 teams play each other in a round-robin system.
      • Top 4 teams qualify for playoffs.
      • 2 qualifiers, 1 eliminator, and a final decide the champion.
    2. Player acquisition, squad composition and salaries
      • Player auction held before each season.
      • Teams can retain players from previous seasons.
      • Salary cap system in place to ensure financial fairness.
    3. Match rules
      • Each match consists of 20 overs per side.
      • Powerplay, death overs, and strategic timeouts are part of the rules.
      • Umpires and third umpires ensure fair play.
    4. Prize money
      • Prize money awarded to the winning team, runners-up, and individual performers.
      • Financial incentives for teams and players based on performance.

  8. Financials


    1. Title Sponsorship

      2. Vivo, Dream11, and Tata have been title sponsors, contributing significantly to the league's revenue.

    2. Payments to foreign national boards

      3. Foreign players' national boards receive a portion of the players' salaries as per BCCI's agreements.

    3. Brand value,

      4. As of 2025, IPL’s brand value is over $10 billion, making it one of the most valuable sporting leagues in the world.


  9. Broadcasting


    1. Sony and WSG (2008-2017)

      2. Initially broadcasted by Sony Entertainment Television and World Sports Group, with rights later acquired by Star India.

    2. Star India(2018-2022)

      3. Star India held the broadcasting rights, significantly increasing viewership and revenue.Won rights for ₹16,347 crore.

    3. 2023-2027

      4. Digital streaming by JioCinema, TV rights with Star Sports.

    4. International broadcasters

      5. Channels in UK, USA, Middle East, and Australia air the matches globally.
